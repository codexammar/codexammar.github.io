Features & User Guide

DeskOp is a lightweight desktop overlay built with C# WPF that docks to the top or bottom of your screen. It stays beneath all open windows but above your wallpaper, meaning it never gets in your way while working or gaming. Think of it as a smart layer that blends into your desktop ‚Äî always accessible, never intrusive.

Custom Filtering

Easily switch categories like Games, Work, or Docs using the filter buttons. DeskOp reads from filters.json and only shows relevant shortcuts based on keywords. You can even edit this file to define your own categories.

SnapZones

DeskOp can snap cleanly to different parts of your screen using its intuitive SnapZone system. You can place the overlay at the top, bottom, left, or right of your screen ‚Äî each zone intelligently resizes and aligns based on your resolution and content.

Simply select a SnapZone from the settings menu or toggle filters to re-snap dynamically. Side snap zones are vertically centered, while top/bottom zones are horizontally balanced ‚Äî giving you precise control over DeskOp‚Äôs layout without manual dragging.

Theme Customization

Choose a light or dark mode, select your accent color, and DeskOp will remember your style between sessions. Changes are saved in theme-settings.json.

Persistent State

DeskOp remembers your last used filter, filter buttons location, snap location, and theme. Everything is restored when you reboot or launch the app again ‚Äî no setup needed each time.

Customize Filters

Want your own categories? Just open filters.json and add new keywords under a label. DeskOp will match shortcuts using those keywords (e.g., "steam", "pdf", "docs") and show only those in each mode.

How Filtering Works

DeskOp loads all files from your Desktop folder. It then checks each shortcut name for category keywords (defined in filters.json) and displays only those matching the selected filter. The layout auto-sizes itself and adjusts to your screen position.
